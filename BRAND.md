# Haul It All — Brand Foundation

The single source of truth for how Haul It All looks, sounds, and positions itself.
Everything on the site, in ads, on the trucks, and in the app should ladder up to this.

House rules: no em-dashes anywhere in copy (use commas, periods, or parentheses). Customer is the hero, brand is the guide. Lead with the customer's problem and outcome, never with our equipment.

---

## What we are

Residential junk removal and commercial dumpster rental serving the Gainesville / Hall County / Lake Lanier corridor and the north metro Atlanta suburbs.

Two businesses, one brand:
- **Junk removal** (done-for-you): a crew comes and hauls it. Volume-based, urgent, emotional, homeowner-driven. The cash-flow engine.
- **Dumpster rental** (self-service): we drop a container, the customer fills it, we haul it. Flat-rate with an included tonnage allowance. Residential DIY plus the recurring contractor revenue that carries the slow months.

Keep the two spines separate on the site. Different buyers, keywords, and calls to action. Cross-link only where a customer is deciding between them.

## Positioning statement (internal north star)

For busy North Georgia homeowners and contractors who want their space and their time back, Haul It All is the junk removal and dumpster rental company that makes the whole mess disappear without the guesswork: upfront pricing, on-time crews that treat your property like their own, and a promise to donate and recycle everything we can. We are the local, do-it-right alternative to faceless national haulers and the cheapest guy with a trailer.

**Where we sit:** more professional and transparent than the low-price trailer guys, more human and honest than the national brands that will not quote a price until a crew is in your driveway, and more personal than the corporate waste companies you can only reach through a call center.

## Ideal customer

Upper-middle-class families (the "almost rich" / mass affluent, roughly $100k to $250k household income), professionals and dual-income parents, plus local contractors, remodelers, roofers, property managers, and realtors on the commercial side.

What they buy on: **value, not price.** They will pay a premium (real data puts it near 10 to 12 percent) for demonstrated value. So we never undercut. The premium is the experience: uniformed, respectful crews, on-time arrival, proactive communication, upfront transparent pricing, spotless cleanup, and a guarantee. Premium, not luxury. No velvet-rope language.

The eco angle is a genuine wedge with this customer (millennials and parents over-index on willingness to pay for sustainability), but it is the tiebreaker, not the lead. Lead with effortless and honest, close with responsible. Make it specific and provable (what we donate, where, how much we divert, donation receipts), never vague, because this audience is alert to greenwashing.

## StoryBrand script

- **Hero:** a busy North Georgia homeowner or contractor who wants the mess gone and their time back.
- **Problem (external):** too much junk, or a project that needs a dumpster, and no time or truck to deal with it.
- **Problem (internal):** it is overwhelming and easy to put off; they do not want to be nickel-and-dimed, wait all day for a no-show, or hand their home to a stranger.
- **Problem (philosophical):** reclaiming your space should be easy and honest, and it should not trash the planet.
- **Guide (us):** empathy ("nobody wants to spend their weekend at the dump") plus authority (licensed, insured, hundreds of local jobs, strong reviews, real proof of what we divert from the landfill).
- **Plan (three steps):** 1) Get your price up front. 2) We show up on time and do all the heavy lifting. 3) It is gone, and gone responsibly.
- **Call to action:** Direct: Get My Price / Book Now. Transitional: See Pricing / Get a Free Quote.
- **Failure:** another lost weekend, a driveway full of junk, a flaky no-show, surprise fees, old stuff rotting in a landfill.
- **Success:** space reclaimed, weekend back, a fair price known going in, zero stress, and the good feeling that it was donated and recycled.

---

## Three messaging pillars (in order)

1. **Effortless.** We do everything. Book in minutes, same-day and next-day available, our crew does all the lifting and cleanup, or we drop a dumpster right where you need it.
2. **Honest and on time.** Upfront flat pricing, no surprise fees. A text when we are on the way and a photo once your dumpster is placed. Licensed, insured, on time, or we make it right.
3. **Responsible.** We donate and recycle everything we can, keep it out of the landfill, and hand you a donation receipt.

Lead with 1 and 2, close with 3.

## Voice

Warm, confident, plain-spoken, with a light touch of fun. Talk like a genuinely helpful neighbor who happens to be great at this. Never corporate, never so cutesy it reads as unserious. Customer is always the hero of the sentence. No jargon. No em-dashes. Sentence case for buttons and headings.

Differentiation lines (ads / "why us"):
- Versus national brands: real prices up front, not a crew guessing in your driveway.
- Versus the cheapest trailer guy: on time, insured, and your driveway stays clean.
- Versus the corporate waste companies: a local you can actually call or text, not a call center.

## Visual identity

### Color tokens
| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `paper` | `#FBFAF4` | Page background. Warm off-white, premium, faint recycled-paper feel. |
| `ink` | `#15140E` | Body text, bold blocks. Warm near-black, not sterile. |
| `haul` | `#6FC23A` | The logo lime. The pop. Fills, CTAs (with ink text), big graphic moments. |
| `haul-bright` | `#86D64A` | Lighter lime for highlights and hovers. |
| `forest` | `#2C6B1C` | Accessible green for green text and small elements on light. |
| `kraft` | `#EDE7D3` | Recycled-paper tan for eco surfaces and secondary panels. |

Contrast rule: bright `haul` green always pairs with `ink` (black) text, echoing the logo (black cap on green). For green text on white, use `forest`.

### Type
- **Display:** Bricolage Grotesque (characterful, warm, confident).
- **Body:** Hanken Grotesk (friendly, legible).
- **Utility / labels / stats:** JetBrains Mono (industrial stamp, used sparingly for eyebrows and numbers).

### Signature elements
- **Sticker / patch motif:** the bear comes from sticker culture, so trust badges and eco callouts render as slightly rotated rounded patches with a bold outline. Use with restraint (one or two moments per page).
- **Area ticker:** a rolling horizontal marquee of service-area names, like a banner down the side of a truck. Doubles as a local SEO signal. Respects prefers-reduced-motion.

### Logo note (important)
The current mascot is very close to the Grateful Dead "dancing bear," which is actively trademarked and licensed. Before we pour SEO and ad money into this brand, commission an original bear that owns the same energy (dancing, recycling cap, green/black/white) without tracing the Dead silhouette. The color scheme and eco-bear concept are ours to keep; the specific silhouette is the risk. Drop the final logo at `public/logo.svg` (and `public/logo.png` for social). Until then the site uses a type wordmark and an original recycling badge so nothing breaks.
